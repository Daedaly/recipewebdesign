Breakfast

Our first recipe is a basic omelet

An omelet is your basic egg tortilla. You can put anything in it that you would like to eat.

Time: 5-10 minutes

Ingredients:

  1. EGGS!?!
  2. Frying pan for eggs
  3. 1 egg flipping spatula

Total price for "project"

  1. $1.29 considering that you have a frying pan and spatula

How to cook:

  1. Get a frying pan and pour a very little amount of vegetable oil into the pan.
  2. Crack 2-3 eggs into the pan and throw away the shells
  3. Set temperature on stove to Medium(that depends on how hot your stove goes to)and set the eggs simmer into a "pancake" like shape till it forms the shape. Here, it's your choice to put anything like bacon, cheese, etc.
  4. Once one side has cooked, flip the entire side of the tortilla and cook the other side.
  5. Then when it is ready, turn off the heat, be careful it can be hot, but get the omlet tortilla nd put it on a plate and fold it in half and serve it with whatever floats your boat! Strawberries, cream cheese, etc.
  6. And BOOM, you got yourself a perfect omelet!

Our next recipe will be: Man's favorite, Scrambled Eggs!

To do this, you will require a few more things.

Time: 3-5 minutes

Ingredients:

  1. Eggs
  2. Frying pan
  3. Spatula

Cooking the scrambled mastery

  • You need to get the frying pan and pour a little bit of vegetable oil.
  • Crack 2-3 eggs(or more, depending on how many people are being served)
  • Add a little bit of butter in the pan to sweeten the batch
  • Here's where it gets weird. Add a little bit of milk to the batch, the more milk, the more fluffy your eggs get!
  • Make sure to throughly mix around the batch and let it fry along.
    • Once they are ready, and not liquidy any more, go ahead and turn the heat off and serve on a plate, be careful, the pan can be hot.

    Avgolemono(Greek Lemon-Egg Soup)

    Ingredients:

    1. 6 cups low-sodium chicken broth
    2. 3/4 cups of long-grain rice
    3. 2 cooked chicken-breast halves, skin discarded and meat shreddded
    4. 2 large eggs, at room temperature
      5. 1/3 cup fresh lemon juice
    5. 1 lemon, thinly sliced

    Time to cook: 15-16 minutes

    How to cook:

    1. Place the broth and the rice in a large saucepan and bring to a boil.
    2. Reduce to a simmer, cover, and cook for 15 minutes or until the rice is cooked.
    3. Add the chicken. Remove the soup from heat and cover.
    4. Whisk the eggs and lemon juice in a medium bowl until frothy.
    5. Slowly add about 1 cup hot chicken broth to the lemon and eggs, whisking continually. Slowly stir the warmed eggs into the soup.
    6. Ladle soup into bowls. Serve immediately with a slice of lemon.

    Potato, Ham, and Spinach Fritata

    Ingredients

    1. 3 tablespoons of olive oil
    2. 2 small russet potatoes (about 3/4 pound), peeled and thinly sliced
      3. 1 small onion, thinly sliced
    3. 9 large eggs
      4. salt and black pepper
    4. 10-ounce package of frozen chopped spinach, thawed and squeezed of excess liquid
    5. 4 ounces white Cheddar, grated (1 cup)
    6. 4 ounces of thunly sliced deli ham, cut into 2-inch pieces
    7. 1 tablespoon of red wine vinegar
    8. 1 head of red leaf lettuce, torn into bite-size pieces (6 cups)

    Time to Cook: 35 minutes and serves 4

    How to cook

    1. Heat oven to 400° F. Heat 2 tablespoons of the oil in a large ovenproof nonstick skillet over medium heat. Add the potatoes and onion and cook, tossing occasionally, until the potatoes are tender, 12 to 15 minutes.
    2. Meanwhile, in a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, ½ teaspoon salt, and ¼ teaspoon pepper. Mix in the spinach, Cheddar, and ham.
    3. Add the egg mixture to the skillet, stir once, and transfer the skillet to oven. Cook until the eggs are set, 12 to 14 minutes.
    4. Meanwhile, in a large bowl, whisk together the vinegar, mustard, the remaining tablespoon of oil, and ¼ teaspoon each salt and pepper. Add the lettuce and toss to coat. Serve with the frittata.

    Dinner recipes

    First one will be a Skillet Soufflé

    Ingredients

    1. 6 large eggs, separated
    2. 1/4 cup chopped fresh chives
    3. 1 teaspoon salt
    4. 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
    5. 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
    6. 4 ounces goat cheese
    7. 1 5-ounce bag salad greens
    8. 1/2 pint grape tomatoes, halved
    9. 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
    10. 1 lemon, cut into wedges

    Time to cook: 15 minutes

    How to cook:

    1. Heat oven to 400° F.
    2. In a large bowl, whisk together the egg yolks, chives, salt, and pepper.
    3. In a separate bowl, with an electric mixer on medium-high speed, beat the egg whites until stiff peaks form. Using a spatula, gently fold the whites into the yolk mixture.
    4. Melt the butter in a large nonstick skillet over medium-low heat and tilt to coat the sides.
    5. Add the egg mixture to the skillet and crumble the cheese over the top. Bake until the eggs are puffed and golden, about 10 minutes.
    6. Cut into wedges. Divide the soufflé, salad greens, and tomatoes among plates. Drizzle the greens with the oil and serve with the lemon.

    Next recipe will be a Brown Rice Pasta, Asparagus, and Eggs

    Time to cook: 25 minutes

    Ingredients

    1. 8 ounces brown rice spaghetti
    2. 1 pound asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
    3. 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
    4. 4 large eggs, beaten
    5. 1 15-ounce can chickpeas, rinsed
    6. 1/4 cup grated Parmesan
    7. 1/4 cup chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley
    8. 2 tablespoon olive oil
    9. salt and black pepper

    How to Cook

    1. Cook the pasta according to the package directions, adding the asparagus for the last 3 minutes of cooking time. Drain and return both to the pot.
    2. Meanwhile, heat the butter in a medium nonstick skillet over medium heat. Add the eggs and scramble until set, 3 to 4 minutes.
    3. Add the eggs to the pasta along with the chickpeas, Parmesan, parsley, oil, ½ teaspoon salt, and ¼ teaspoon pepper and toss to combine.

    Desserts

    The first recipe will be a Cranberry-Orange Trifle Recipe

    Time to cook: 1 hour, makes 16 servings

    Ingredients:

      Main items

    1. 2 packages (12 ounces each) fresh or frozen cranberries
    2. 1-1/2 cups water
    3. 1-1/3 cups sugar
    4. 2 teaspoons minced fresh gingerroot
    5. 4 teaspoons grated orange peel

      6. Custard

    6. 1 cup sugar
    7. 2 tablespoons cornstarch
    8. 1/4 teaspoon salt
    9. 3 cups 2% milk
    10. 6 egg yolks
    11. 2 teaspoons vanilla extract

      12. Trifle

    12. 1 loaf (16 ounces) frozen pound cake, thawed and cut into 1-inch cubes
    13. 1/4 cup orange liqueur
    14. 1/2 cup slivered almonds, toasted
    15. Sweetened whipped cream and orange sections

    How to Cook

    1. In a large saucepan, combine the first five ingredients; bring to a boil, stirring to dissolve sugar. Reduce heat to medium; cook, uncovered, until berries pop and mixture is thickened, about 15 minutes. Remove from the heat; cool completely.
    2. For custard, in a large saucepan, mix the sugar, cornstarch and salt. Whisk in milk. Cook and stir over medium-high heat until thickened and bubbly. Reduce heat to low; cook and stir 2 minutes longer. Remove from the heat.
    3. In a small bowl, whisk a small amount of hot milk mixture into egg yolks; return all to the pan, whisking constantly. Bring to a gentle boil; cook and stir for 2 minutes. Immediately transfer to a clean bowl; stir in vanilla. Cool for 30 minutes. Press waxed paper onto surface of filling; refrigerate until cold, about 1 hour.
    4. To assemble, place half of the cake on the bottom of a 4-qt. trifle bowl or glass bowl; drizzle with 2 tablespoons orange liqueur. Layer with half of the cranberry mixture, 1/4 cup almonds and half of the custard. Repeat layers. Refrigerate, covered, until serving. Top with whipped cream and orange sections. Yield: 16 servings (1 cup each).

    And the Last recipe will be a Caramelized Orange Pumpkin Flan

    Time to cook: 6 hours, 15 minutes

    Ingredients

    1. 1 2/3 cups sugar, divided
    2. 1/8 teaspoon cream of tartar
    3. Zest of 2 large oranges, divided
    4. 5 large eggs
    5. 1 1/2 cups half-and-half
    6. 1/2 cup sour cream
    7. 1/2 teaspoon ground mace or nutmeg
    8. 1/2 teaspoon ground allspice
    9. 1/4 teaspoon salt
    10. 1 cup Homemade Pumpkin Puree

    How to cook:

    1. Preheat oven to 350° and put a 2 1/4- to 2 1/2-qt. soufflé dish in oven to heat.
    2. Cook 1 cup sugar, 1/4 cup water, and the cream of tartar in a large frying pan over medium-high heat, stirring occasionally, until sugar turns deep golden, 8 to 12 minutes. Remove from heat and stir in zest of 1 orange. Protecting hands, remove dish from oven and pour caramelized sugar inside. Tilt dish until syrup thickens and evenly coats bottom and 1 to 2 in. up side.
    3. Heat a kettle of water to boiling. In a bowl, whisk remaining 2/3 cup sugar and zest of 1 orange with remaining ingredients. Pour into soufflé dish and set dish in a 9-in. square baking pan. Put pan with dish on oven rack. Pour boiling water into pan halfway up side of soufflé dish.
    4. Bake until a knife inserted halfway between center and edge comes out clean, 1 1/4 to 1 1/2 hours. Remove dish from water and let cool on a rack, then chill, covered, at least 4 hours.
    5. Run a thin metal spatula around inside of dish, then invert onto a deep plate. Set soufflé dish in hot water 10 minutes to loosen syrup (not all will come off); scrape syrup over flan. Cut flan into wedges.
